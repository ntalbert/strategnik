## Post 1: Insight Post

69% of Google searches now end without a click. ChatGPT just crossed 900 million weekly users.

Here's the part most B2B teams haven't internalized yet: your buyer's first interaction with your product category is increasingly happening inside an AI-generated answer, not on your website.

That means the old funnel -- ad, click, landing page, form fill -- has a new competitor. And it's not another vendor. It's a summary paragraph that already answered the question your content was supposed to answer.

The companies I'm working with that are ahead of this aren't "doing AEO" as a project. They're restructuring how they think about content entirely -- building for citation, not just traffic.

The shift isn't coming. It's already reshaping pipeline attribution in ways most dashboards can't see yet.

## Post 3: Client Observation Post

Pattern I've noticed across three engagements this quarter:

The team is producing content consistently. Blog posts, LinkedIn, even some gated stuff. Leadership feels good about the volume. But when you ask "which of these pieces contributed to a closed deal?" -- silence.

Not because the content is bad. Because nobody built the connective tissue between publishing and pipeline. No UTM discipline. No intent signal mapping. No feedback loop from sales on what prospects actually reference.

Content without attribution infrastructure is just brand therapy. It might make you feel productive, but you can't defend the budget when the board asks what it's generating.

The fix isn't more content. It's instrumenting what you already have so you can see what's working and kill what isn't.

The Market

A $100–400M Greenfield Hiding Inside a Bigger Market

The global freight audit and payment (FAP) market ranges from $650M to $1.6B, growing at 9–17% CAGR toward $1.5–3.5B by 2030. But these figures obscure a critical segmentation reality: the FAP market is overwhelmingly shipper-dominated. The major incumbents — Cass, CTSI-Global, nVision Global, Trax — all serve shippers. None were built for the broker back-office workflow.

Navix occupies the single most defensible position in a $100–400M addressable market that no competitor has bothered to claim. Every rival treats audit as a secondary feature bolted onto payments, factoring, or document management. Navix is the only AI-native, audit-first platform handling LTL, drayage, and FTL in one tool — yet it remains invisible in every channel that drives modern B2B discovery.

The buying triggers are active now. The 2022–2025 freight recession compressed margins severely — top-10 brokerage revenue fell 18% YoY, C.H. Robinson cut headcount 10.8%, J.B. Hunt's ICS division reported $7M in losses through Q3 2025.

Private equity is the dominant trigger. PE firms have bid up freight brokerage EBITDA multiples to 12–14x, creating intense pressure on portfolio companies to optimize operations. Add-on acquisition activity doubled year-over-year from 10 deals in 2023 to 20 in 2024, each deal creating a technology evaluation window.

Navix's ROI story: 2–7% freight spend recovery, up to 85% reduction in invoice processing costs, 3–10 day DSO reduction, 50–70% labor reallocation, 8-week implementation.

Gap 2: Zero LLM Citations

+
Status
Navix does not appear in ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, or Google AI Overviews for any freight audit query. The LLM citation landscape is dominated entirely by shipper-focused incumbents.
How LLMs Decide
Only 12% of pages ranking #1–3 on Google get cited by ChatGPT. 72.4% of cited pages contain "answer capsules" (120–150 char responses after H2 headings). LLMs look for consensus across 3+ independent sources. 67% of top cited pages come from original research.
Impact
B2B buyers increasingly use AI for product research. Every month this gap persists, AI models learn to recommend competitors instead. This is an existential risk as AI-mediated discovery replaces traditional research.
Fix
Requires systematic closure of all downstream gaps — review platforms, comparison content, case studies, and earned media create the multi-source consensus LLMs need.
